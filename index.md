# About Me

I am currently pursuing my Ph.D. in Computer Science at Purdue University under the guidance of [Prof. Antonio Bianchi](https://antoniobianchi.me/). I completed my Master of Science in Computer Science at Binghamton University, SUNY, where I was recognized as a Distinguished Graduate Student. Prior to that, I earned my Bachelor of Science in Information Security from Renmin University of China. My research focuses on program analysis, binary analysis, and reverse engineering, with a particular emphasis on leveraging large language models and symbolic execution to verify patch safety, generate human-readable descriptions of binary patches, and enhance vulnerability management for both modern and legacy embedded systems.

# Program Contributions
- **[DARPA AI Cyber Challenge (AIxCC)](https://aicyberchallenge.com/):** Spearheaded the development of *Artiphishell* with team [Shellphish](https://shellphish.net/) — an LLM-based Cyber Reasoning System that autonomously discovers and patches vulnerabilities. Won a $2M semifinal award and placed 5th in the final at DEF CON 33 (out of 42 initial teams). [\[blog\]](https://support.shellphish.net/blog/2025/08/22/shellphish-x-aixcc-pm/)
- **[DARPA Assured Micropatching (AMP)](https://www.darpa.mil/research/programs/assured-micropatching):** Applied and extended *VeriBin* to verify binary-level micropatches on legacy embedded systems (Cummins engine ECUs, NASA lunar rovers, power-grid controllers), enhancing angr's symbolic execution across ARM, PowerPC, and other embedded architectures.
